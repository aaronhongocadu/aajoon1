The two articles clearly raise the problem of commercialization and monopolization. It is argued that digital users, especially graphic designers, need to reduce their dependence on Adobe and Apple due to the autonomy of software and hardware. The view is that the definition of the term 'graphic designing' itself is turning into the 'using of Adobe and Apple's products', and argues that a solution should be sought immediately so that digital world/industries can be used more healthily and diversely. Usually, people think highly of software dependence when they think of 'designers', but in fact, they are more aware of the importance of hardware as one of the 'designers'. Because of the connectivity between software and hardware, it is natural that designing something requires both, and hardware most likely exists for software, but new hardware is much more important than people think for the designers at this time when some multiple designer software requires high-end hardware. Even if it is not due to environmental issues, I think recyclable resources are becoming increasingly essential for the designers in the industries, and the objects they design are naturally turing into recycling-friendly.
